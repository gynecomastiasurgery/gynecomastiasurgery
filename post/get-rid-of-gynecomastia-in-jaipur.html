Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

Gynecomastia surgery is performed under anesthesia, ensuring that you won’t experience pain during the procedure itself. After surgery, some discomfort or mild pain is normal, but this can be effectively managed with prescribed pain medications. The overall goal is to minimize any discomfort while ensuring safe and successful results.

How long is the recovery period?

Recovery periods can vary based on individual factors, such as the extent of the surgery and your body’s natural healing process. However, most patients can expect to resume light activities within about a week after the procedure. It’s advisable to avoid strenuous activities for a few weeks to allow your body sufficient time to heal properly and achieve the best results.

Will there be noticeable scars?

Dr. Purohit’s surgical techniques are designed to minimize scarring. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas to ensure that any resulting scars are as discreet as possible. Over time, these scars tend to fade, becoming less noticeable and allowing you to enjoy the results of the surgery without significant visible markings.

Are the results of gynecomastia surgery permanent?

Yes, in most cases, the results of gynecomastia surgery are permanent. The excess breast tissue and fat removed during the procedure are unlikely to return. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet, can help you maintain the results over the long term. It’s important to follow post-operative instructions and attend follow-up appointments to ensure the best outcome.

Are there any risks associated with the surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia surgery does carry some risks. During your consultation with Dr. Purohit, he will discuss these potential risks and complications with you.
