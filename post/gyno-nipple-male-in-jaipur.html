The Science Behind Gynecomastia

Understanding gynecomastia begins with comprehending its underlying mechanisms. This condition arises due to an imbalance between estrogen (often considered a “female” hormone) and testosterone (a “male” hormone) levels. Contrary to misconceptions, it’s not just an issue that affects overweight men; it can occur in individuals of various body types. Gynecomastia can emerge during puberty, as a side effect of certain medications, due to hormonal imbalances, or even as a natural part of aging.

Q: Is gynecomastia surgery safe?

Absolutely. Gynecomastia surgery, when performed by a skilled and experienced surgeon, is a safe procedure. Our team in Jaipur follows rigorous safety protocols and ensures that you receive the highest standard of care before, during, and after the surgery.

Q: What’s the recovery time after gyno surgery?

Recovery time varies from person to person, but generally, most patients can resume light activities within a week after surgery. Q: Will there be visible scars?

While some scarring is inevitable with any surgical procedure, I take utmost care to minimize their visibility. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas. Q: Can weight loss alone eliminate gynecomastia?

Weight loss might help reduce the appearance of gynecomastia if excess fat contributes to the condition. However, if glandular tissue is the primary cause, surgical intervention is often required for its removal. During our consultations in Jaipur, we evaluate the underlying causes to determine the most suitable approach.

Q: Are the results of gyno surgery permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are typically permanent, especially when glandular tissue is removed. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and balanced nutrition, can help preserve the results over the long term.
