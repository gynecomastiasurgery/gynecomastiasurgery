Types of Gynecomastia: A Closer Look

Exploring Gynecomastia Types in Jaipur

Gynecomastia is not a one-size-fits-all condition; it presents in various forms based on factors such as its underlying cause, tissue composition, and severity. In Jaipur, individuals dealing with gynecomastia have distinct types that I, Dr. Vishal Purohit, am equipped to address. Understanding the types of gynecomastia is crucial in determining the most suitable treatment approach.

1. Hormonal Gynecomastia: This type is often associated with imbalances in hormone levels, particularly an increase in estrogen or a decrease in testosterone. Hormonal gynecomastia is common during puberty and can lead to temporary breast tissue enlargement in males.

2. Pseudogynecomastia: Unlike hormonal gynecomastia, pseudogynecomastia is primarily caused by excess body fat rather than glandular tissue growth. This type can often be improved through lifestyle modifications such as diet and exercise. 3. True Gynecomastia: True gynecomastia involves the enlargement of glandular tissue in the male breast. It can result from various factors, including hormonal imbalances, genetics, medications, and medical conditions. 4. Asymmetrical Gynecomastia: Asymmetrical gynecomastia refers to cases where one breast is larger than the other. This can stem from uneven distribution of glandular tissue and fat. 5. Severe Gynecomastia: Severe gynecomastia is characterized by substantial enlargement of the breast tissue, often causing discomfort and self-consciousness. Surgical intervention is frequently necessary to address this type effectively. To illustrate the differences among these gynecomastia types, consider the following table:
