Surgical Techniques

Chest gynecomastia surgery typically involves the removal of excess breast tissue and contouring of the chest for a more masculine appearance. The procedure is performed under general anesthesia to ensure your comfort throughout. Depending on the extent of tissue development and skin laxity, I may employ one of the following techniques:

  • Liposuction: In cases where excess fat is the primary concern, liposuction is utilized to remove the unwanted fat deposits. This technique involves minimal incisions and is particularly effective for patients with good skin elasticity.
  • Excision: For cases involving excess glandular tissue or sagging skin, excision techniques are employed. This involves the removal of excess tissue and skin through discreetly placed incisions, resulting in a well-contoured chest.

Recovery and Results

Following the surgery, a compression garment is worn to support the healing process and minimize swelling. Most patients can resume light activities within a week, with gradual return to more strenuous activities over the next few weeks. The final results of the surgery become apparent as the swelling subsides, revealing a more sculpted and confident chest.

FAQs About Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

As you embark on your journey to address gynecomastia concerns, it’s natural to have questions about the procedure, recovery, and results. In this section, I’ll address five commonly asked questions to provide you with clarity and peace of mind.

1. What causes gynecomastia?

Gynecomastia can be triggered by a variety of factors, including hormonal imbalances, certain medications, genetics, and lifestyle choices. It results in the enlargement of male breast tissue and can lead to discomfort and self-consciousness. During our consultations, I thoroughly assess the underlying causes to determine the most appropriate treatment approach for each individual.

2. Is the procedure painful?

While discomfort is common after gynecomastia surgery, it is typically manageable and temporary. Pain medication will be prescribed to alleviate any discomfort during the initial stages of recovery. As the healing process progresses, any discomfort will gradually subside.

3. Are the results permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent, provided that a healthy lifestyle is maintained. It’s important to maintain a stable weight and follow post-operative instructions to ensure the longevity of the results. Any concerns or questions regarding long-term results can be discussed during our consultations.

4. When can I resume regular activities?

Most patients can resume light activities within a week after surgery. However, it’s advisable to avoid strenuous exercises and heavy lifting for a few weeks to ensure proper healing. I will provide you with detailed guidelines to facilitate a smooth recovery and optimal results.

5. Are there any potential risks?

As with any surgical procedure, there are minimal risks associated with gynecomastia surgery. These risks include infection, bleeding, scarring, and changes in nipple sensation. However, I take every precaution to minimize these risks by employing advanced surgical techniques and providing comprehensive pre-operative and post-operative care.
