Distinguishing Between True Gynecomastia and Pseudo Gynecomastia

Understanding the underlying causes of gynecomastia is essential for effective treatment. True gynecomastia involves the growth of glandular breast tissue, often resulting from hormonal imbalances. Pseudo gynecomastia, on the other hand, is characterized by excess fat deposits in the chest area, giving the appearance of enlarged breasts. Accurate diagnosis is key to determining the most appropriate treatment approach.

Tailored Treatments for Lasting Results

Dr. Vishal Purohit’s clinic offers a range of treatments to address both true gynecomastia and pseudo gynecomastia. For true gynecomastia cases, surgical intervention may be recommended to remove excess glandular tissue and restore a more masculine chest contour. Liposuction may also be utilized to target excess fat in the chest area. Pseudo gynecomastia, primarily caused by excess fat, can often be effectively treated with liposuction alone.

The recovery period after gynecomastia surgery can vary based on the extent of the procedure, individual healing factors, and the surgical techniques used. 2. What are the risks associated with gyno surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, gyno surgery carries potential risks, although they are rare. These risks may include infection, bleeding, scarring, asymmetry, and changes in nipple sensation. Dr. Purohit’s expertise and commitment to safety mitigate these risks. During your consultation, he will provide you with detailed information about potential risks and how they can be minimized.

3. Is gynecomastia surgery a permanent solution?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery provides a permanent solution for the removal of excess glandular tissue and fat deposits. Once removed, these tissues do not grow back. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle is essential to prevent the recurrence of excess fat deposits. Dr. Vishal Purohit’s clinic emphasizes the importance of a balanced diet and regular exercise to ensure lasting results.

5. Can pseudo gynecomastia be treated without surgery?

Yes, pseudo gynecomastia, which is primarily caused by excess fat deposits, can often be effectively treated without surgery.
