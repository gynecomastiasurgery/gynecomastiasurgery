Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Here are answers to some of the most commonly asked questions about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur:

1. Is gynecomastia surgery safe?

Absolutely. Patient safety is my top priority. Gynecomastia surgery is generally safe when performed by a skilled and experienced plastic surgeon. I take every precaution to ensure a safe surgical environment, including thorough pre-operative assessments and proper post-operative care. Rest assured that I am dedicated to providing the highest level of safety and care throughout your surgical journey.

2. What is the typical recovery period?

Recovery times can vary based on the extent of the procedure and individual healing rates. Generally, you can expect to resume light activities within about a week after surgery. Strenuous activities and exercise should be avoided for a few weeks to ensure proper healing. I provide detailed post-operative instructions to guide you through a smooth recovery and help you achieve the best possible results.

3. Will there be visible scarring?

I prioritize minimizing scarring by strategically placing incisions in inconspicuous locations. Over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable. I use advanced techniques to ensure that scarring is as minimal as possible, while still achieving the desired surgical outcomes.

4. Can non-surgical treatments provide significant results?

Non-surgical treatments can be effective for mild cases of gynecomastia. However, for more significant cases that involve excess glandular tissue or fat, surgical intervention might be a more suitable option. During our consultation, I will assess your condition and discuss the best treatment approach to help you achieve your desired results.

5. Are the results permanent?

Gynecomastia surgery can provide long-lasting results, especially when paired with a healthy lifestyle. However, maintaining a stable weight and following a balanced diet and exercise routine are essential for sustaining the outcomes. While the aging process will naturally continue, the improvements achieved through surgery should be enduring.
