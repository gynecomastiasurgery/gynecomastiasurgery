Types of Gynaecomastia in Jaipur

Gynaecomastia comes in various forms, each with distinct characteristics and underlying causes. Understanding the specific type of gynaecomastia you’re dealing with is pivotal in tailoring an effective treatment plan that addresses your unique needs. As an experienced plastic surgeon, I’m here to shed light on the different types of gynaecomastia prevalent in Jaipur.

Types of Gynaecomastia:

  1. Pseudogynecomastia: This type is primarily caused by excess fat accumulation in the chest area, without significant glandular tissue enlargement. It is often linked to lifestyle factors, such as weight gain and lack of exercise. Liposuction is an effective approach for addressing this type.
  2. True Gynaecomastia: True gynaecomastia involves the enlargement of both glandular tissue and fat in the chest area. Hormonal imbalances, medications, and certain medical conditions can contribute to its development. Surgical excision and liposuction are commonly employed to treat this type.
  3. Unilateral Gynaecomastia: When only one side of the chest is affected, it’s termed unilateral gynaecomastia. This can result from a variety of causes, including hormonal imbalances, genetics, or even an underlying medical condition.
  4. Bilateral Gynaecomastia: Bilateral gynaecomastia refers to the enlargement of both breasts in a symmetrical manner. Hormonal fluctuations during puberty or imbalances later in life can trigger this type.

FAQs: Commonly Asked Questions about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

Here, I’ve compiled answers to frequently asked questions about gynecomastia surgery to provide you with a clear understanding of the procedure and its various aspects.

What causes gynecomastia?

Gynecomastia can be triggered by various factors:

  • Hormonal Imbalances: Fluctuations in hormone levels, particularly an increase in estrogen or a decrease in testosterone, can lead to breast tissue growth in males.
  • Genetics: Family history can contribute to the likelihood of developing gynecomastia.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as those for hypertension, prostate conditions, and certain psychiatric drugs, can cause breast tissue enlargement.
  • Underlying Health Conditions: Conditions like obesity, hypogonadism, and thyroid disorders can contribute to gynecomastia.

Is gynecomastia surgery safe?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery is generally safe when performed by a skilled plastic surgeon. Safety is a top priority in all surgical procedures. Before proceeding with surgery, I conduct a thorough assessment of your health and medical history to ensure that you are a suitable candidate for the procedure. The surgery is performed in a controlled and sterile environment to minimize risks.

How long is the recovery period after surgery?

Recovery times can vary based on the extent of the surgery and individual healing rates. Most patients can resume normal activities within a week or two after the surgery. Strenuous activities and heavy lifting may need to be avoided for a few weeks. It’s important to follow post-operative care instructions to ensure optimal healing and results.

Will there be visible scars after surgery?

I employ advanced surgical techniques to minimize scarring. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas, and they often fade over time. While some scarring may be present initially, they tend to become less noticeable as the body heals. Following proper wound care and avoiding sun exposure can contribute to scar fading.

Can gynecomastia recur after surgery?

In most cases, the results of gynecomastia surgery are permanent. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet and regular exercise, can help prevent future weight gain and potential recurrence. It’s important to discuss any concerns with me during the consultation so that I can provide guidance tailored to your individual situation.
