Frequently Asked Questions about Gynecomastia Surgery

As a seasoned Mch Plastic Surgeon in Jaipur specializing in gynecomastia surgery, I frequently encounter questions from individuals seeking information about the procedure. Here are five of the most commonly asked questions along with detailed answers to help you make an informed decision:

1. What is the recovery time after gyno surgery?

Recovery time after gynecomastia surgery varies depending on the extent of the procedure. Generally, patients can expect to resume light activities within a week. However, more strenuous activities and exercise should be avoided for about 3 to 4 weeks. It’s important to follow post-operative instructions diligently to ensure a smooth recovery.

2. Are the results of the surgery permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are typically permanent. Once the excess glandular tissue and fat are removed, the treated area should remain flatter and more contoured. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle and managing your weight can contribute to long-lasting results.

3. Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

While some discomfort is normal after surgery, pain is generally well managed with prescribed medications. Most patients report mild to moderate discomfort, which subsides as the healing process progresses.

4. What risks are associated with the procedure?

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia surgery carries certain risks, although they are minimal when performed by a skilled surgeon. Potential risks include infection, bleeding, scarring, and asymmetry. During the consultation, we’ll discuss these risks in detail, ensuring you have a comprehensive understanding.

5. Can exercise alone eliminate male breast enlargement?

Exercise can help reduce overall body fat, including in the chest area, but it might not be sufficient to eliminate glandular tissue. If gynecomastia is caused by excess glandular tissue, surgical intervention may be necessary to achieve the desired results.
