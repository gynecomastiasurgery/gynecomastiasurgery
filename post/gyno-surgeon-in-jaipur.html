As a seasoned gynecomastia surgery specialist in Jaipur, I recognize the importance of addressing common concerns and questions about the procedure. 1. What causes gynecomastia and when should I consider surgery?

Gynecomastia can arise due to hormonal imbalances, genetics, medication side effects, and certain medical conditions. It often leads to the enlargement of breast tissue in males. If you find that this condition is causing emotional distress, affecting your self-esteem, or hindering your daily life, it might be time to consider gynecomastia surgery. A personalized consultation with me can help determine if surgery is the right option for you.

2. Is the surgery performed under general anesthesia?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery is typically performed under general anesthesia to ensure your comfort and safety throughout the procedure. Anesthesia allows you to be pain-free and asleep during the surgery. My team and I prioritize patient well-being, and every precaution is taken to ensure a smooth and worry-free surgical experience.

3. What is the recovery timeline after gynecomastia surgery?

Recovery timelines can vary based on individual factors, but generally, you can expect to resume light activities within a week. Strenuous activities and exercise should be avoided for a few weeks to allow for proper healing. Wearing a compression garment is usually recommended during the initial recovery phase to support the healing process and minimize swelling.

4. Are there any potential risks or complications associated with the surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia surgery carries some inherent risks, including infection, bleeding, scarring, and anesthesia-related complications. However, by choosing an experienced gynecomastia surgery specialist and following post-operative instructions diligently, these risks can be minimized. During your consultation, I will thoroughly discuss potential risks and the steps taken to ensure your safety.

5. Can gynecomastia reoccur after surgical removal?

In most cases, gynecomastia surgery provides lasting results. However, factors like significant weight gain, hormonal imbalances, or underlying medical conditions could potentially contribute to a recurrence. Following a healthy lifestyle, maintaining a stable weight, and adhering to post-operative instructions can significantly reduce the chances of gynecomastia reoccurring.
