FAQs about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

Here are answers to some commonly asked questions about gynecomastia surgery in Jaipur:

Is gynecomastia surgery painful?

Gynecomastia surgery is performed under anesthesia, ensuring that you won’t experience pain during the procedure itself. After the surgery, you might feel some discomfort, swelling, and mild pain, which are common during the recovery phase. However, these effects are manageable with prescribed pain medication and typically subside as you heal.

How long is the recovery period after gynecomastia surgery?

Recovery time can vary based on the extent of the surgery and individual healing factors. In general, most patients can resume light activities within a week after the surgery. However, it’s important to avoid strenuous exercises and heavy lifting for a few weeks to ensure proper healing and optimal results. During your follow-up appointments, I will monitor your progress and guide you on when it’s safe to resume more vigorous activities.

Are the results of gynecomastia surgery permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally permanent, provided that you maintain a healthy lifestyle. However, it’s important to note that significant weight fluctuations or hormonal imbalances in the future could potentially affect the results. By maintaining a stable weight and adopting a healthy lifestyle, you can enjoy the long-lasting benefits of the surgery.

Will there be noticeable scars after gynecomastia surgery?

While gynecomastia surgery does involve incisions, efforts are made to minimize visible scarring. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas to minimize their visibility. Over time, any scars that do form should fade and become less noticeable. Following post-operative care instructions diligently will also help promote optimal healing and minimize the appearance of scars.

Are there any risks associated with gynecomastia surgery?

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia surgery carries minimal risks. These can include infection, bleeding, unfavorable scarring, asymmetry, and changes in nipple sensation. However, choosing an experienced and qualified surgeon, adhering to pre-operative and post-operative care instructions, and maintaining open communication throughout the process can help mitigate these risks and enhance the likelihood of a successful outcome.
