FAQs: Common Questions About Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

As you embark on your journey towards gynecomastia surgery, it’s natural to have questions and seek clarity. Below are answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about gynecomastia surgery in Jaipur:

1. What is the recovery time after gynecomastia surgery?

Recovery times can vary depending on individual factors and the extent of the surgery. In most cases, patients can expect to resume non-strenuous activities within a week or two after the procedure. However, engaging in vigorous physical activities and heavy lifting should be avoided for several weeks. Complete recovery typically occurs within a few weeks.

2. Are the results of gynecomastia surgery permanent?

Yes, the results of gynecomastia surgery are generally long-lasting, provided that you maintain a healthy lifestyle and avoid significant weight fluctuations. The removed glandular tissue and fat deposits are unlikely to return, but it’s important to follow post-operative care instructions to ensure the best outcome.

3. Is gynecomastia surgery safe?

Yes, gynecomastia surgery is considered safe when performed by a skilled and experienced surgeon. As a specialist in this field, I prioritize patient safety and adhere to strict medical standards. During the consultation, I conduct a thorough evaluation of your health and medical history to ensure you are a suitable candidate for the procedure.

4. Will there be visible scars after surgery?

While some scarring is inevitable after any surgery, I utilize advanced surgical techniques to minimize visible scarring as much as possible. Incisions are strategically placed in inconspicuous areas, such as around the edge of the areola. Over time, scars tend to fade and become less noticeable.

5. Can gynecomastia recur after surgery?

In most cases, the breast tissue and fat that are surgically removed during gynecomastia surgery are not likely to return. However, it’s important to maintain a healthy lifestyle to prevent the development of new fat deposits. This includes maintaining a stable weight through a balanced diet and regular exercise.
