FAQs about Gynecomastia Surgery in Jaipur

Here are answers to some common questions patients ask about gynecomastia surgery:

1. Is Gynecomastia Surgery Safe?

Gynecomastia surgery, when performed by a qualified and experienced plastic surgeon, is generally safe. I have years of experience as an Mch Plastic Surgeon in Jaipur and prioritize patient safety throughout the surgical process. Prior to surgery, a thorough evaluation of your health and medical history is conducted to ensure that you are a suitable candidate for the procedure.

2. What is the Typical Recovery Period After Surgery?

The recovery period after gynecomastia surgery varies from person to person. On average, patients can expect to return to work and light activities within a week or two after the surgery. Strenuous physical activities and heavy lifting should be avoided for several weeks to ensure proper healing. I provide personalized aftercare instructions to guide you through a smooth recovery process.

3. Will There Be Visible Scars?

While gynecomastia surgery involves discreet incisions, some scarring is inevitable. However, I strategically place incisions in inconspicuous areas to minimize the visibility of scars. With proper wound care and scar management, scars tend to fade over time and become less noticeable.

4. Are the Results Permanent?

The results of gynecomastia surgery are intended to be permanent. Once the excess glandular tissue and fat are removed, the improved chest contour should endure. However, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet, can contribute to the long-term success of the surgery.

5. Can Gynecomastia Recur After Surgery?

In most cases, gynecomastia surgery provides lasting results. However, factors such as significant weight gain or hormonal changes can potentially contribute to the recurrence of gynecomastia over time. It’s essential to follow post-operative instructions, maintain a stable weight, and attend regular follow-up appointments to monitor your progress.
